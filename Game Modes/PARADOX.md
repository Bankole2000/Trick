# TRICK ENGINE GAME SPECIFICATION

# PARADOX

## Version 1.0

### Classification

Epistemic Game Type:
Self-Reference Game

Primary Psychological Focus:
Logical Stability

Core Question:

> What happens when the game examines itself?

---

# Design Philosophy

PARADOX explores the limits of truth, language, and self-reference.

Many systems fail when they attempt to fully describe themselves.

PARADOX intentionally introduces such situations.

The objective is not certainty.

The objective is navigation.

---

# Psychological Model

PARADOX trains:

* logical reasoning
* contradiction management
* abstraction
* tolerance for uncertainty

The central failure mode is:

> Assuming every question has a stable answer.

---

# Deck Characteristics

Cards contain:

* paradoxes
* self-referential statements
* recursive statements
* contradictory systems

Example cards:

* "This statement is false."
* "The next statement is true."
* "The previous statement is false."

---

# Core Mechanic

Players evaluate statements and then evaluate their own evaluations.

Challenges can target:

* statements
* reasoning
* challenge decisions

The game may intentionally prevent resolution.

Some rounds have no correct answer.

---

# Scoring

Stable Resolution:
+3

Correct Identification of Contradiction:
+3

Recognition of Uncertainty:
+2

False Certainty:
-3

---

# Win Condition

Highest Stability Score.

---

# What It Models

PARADOX examines the limits of:

R
B
U
I
L

and whether complete alignment is always possible.

---

# Ideal Outcome

Players discover:

* not all contradictions are mistakes
* some systems contain unavoidable limits
* uncertainty can be informative

The game rewards intellectual humility over certainty.

---

# Closing Principle

Where ANCHOR asks:

"What is true?"

PARADOX asks:

"What does truth even mean in this situation?"

# TRICK ENGINE GAME SPECIFICATION

# ANCHOR

## Version 1.0

### Classification

Epistemic Game Type:
Truth Stability Game

Primary Psychological Focus:
Reality Alignment

Core Question:

> How accurately can players align their beliefs with reality?

---

# Design Philosophy

ANCHOR is the foundational TRICK game.

It assumes that reality exists independently of belief and that players benefit from improving the accuracy of their internal models.

Unlike other TRICK games, ANCHOR minimizes ambiguity and social manipulation.

The purpose is calibration.

Players are rewarded for being correct, not persuasive.

---

# Psychological Model

ANCHOR trains:

* truth recognition
* uncertainty management
* confidence calibration
* myth correction

The game models how humans construct and update beliefs when exposed to evidence.

The central failure mode is:

> Mistaking confidence for correctness.

---

# Deck Characteristics

Statements should be:

* objectively verifiable
* low ambiguity
* resistant to interpretation disputes

Example categories:

* science
* mathematics
* geography
* biology
* physics

Example cards:

* Water boils at 100°C at sea level.
* Humans only use 10% of their brains.
* The Sun is a star.

---

# Core Mechanic

Players evaluate whether a statement corresponds to reality.

Social influence is minimized.

Reality acts as the primary opponent.

This is primarily a PvE game.

---

# Scoring

Correct Answer:
+2

Incorrect Answer:
-2

Correct Challenge:
+2

Incorrect Challenge:
-1

---

# Win Condition

Highest Reality Alignment Score.

---

# What It Models

ANCHOR models:

R → L

with minimal distortion.

The focus is:

> How accurately can a player discover truth?

rather than

> How effectively can a player influence belief?

---

# Ideal Outcome

Players become better calibrated and more willing to distinguish:

* certainty
* uncertainty
* ignorance

from one another.

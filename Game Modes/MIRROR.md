# TRICK ENGINE GAME SPECIFICATION

# MIRROR

## Version 1.0

### Classification

Epistemic Game Type:
Communication Awareness Game

Primary Psychological Focus:
Interpretation

Core Question:

> How much of communication is actually shared understanding?

---

# Design Philosophy

MIRROR focuses on the gap between what is said and what is understood.

Unlike ANCHOR, the challenge is not discovering reality.

Unlike ECHO, the challenge is not predicting social belief.

The challenge is reconstructing meaning.

Players learn that communication failures often arise not from disagreement about facts but from differences in interpretation.

---

# Psychological Model

MIRROR trains:

* perspective taking
* interpretation awareness
* communication precision
* theory of mind

The central failure mode is:

> Assuming that others interpret words the same way you do.

---

# Deck Characteristics

Statements should be:

* ambiguous
* context-sensitive
* open to multiple interpretations

Example cards:

* "Freedom is important."
* "Success requires sacrifice."
* "Rules create order."

---

# Core Mechanic

After a statement is presented:

1. Speaker privately records intended meaning.
2. Listeners record interpreted meaning.
3. Meanings are revealed.
4. Similarity is scored.

Reality may be irrelevant.

Alignment becomes the primary objective.

---

# Scoring

| Outcome                     | Points |
| --------------------------- | ------ |
| High Interpretation Match   | +3     |
| Moderate Interpretation Match | +1   |
| Low Interpretation Match    | 0      |
| Perfect Group Alignment Bonus | +2   |

---

# Match Rubric

After meanings are revealed, the group votes on match level using the following rubric.

## High Match (+3)

The listener's interpretation and the speaker's intended meaning would lead to the same practical conclusion or action. Core claim, scope, and implication are all substantially aligned.

Example: Speaker means "Freedom requires responsibility." Listener records "Freedom comes with obligations." → High.

## Moderate Match (+1)

The interpretation shares the main claim but diverges in at least one significant dimension — scope, emphasis, or implication — in a way that could lead to a different conclusion in some contexts.

Example: Speaker means "Freedom requires responsibility." Listener records "Freedom is dangerous without rules." → Moderate. Same concern, different framing.

## Low Match (0)

The interpretations diverge enough that they would lead to meaningfully different conclusions or actions. The listener has reconstructed a different proposition.

Example: Speaker means "Freedom requires responsibility." Listener records "Freedom should be unlimited." → Low.

---

# Match Determination Process

1. Speaker privately writes their intended meaning before the statement is discussed.
2. Each listener privately writes their interpreted meaning after hearing it.
3. Speaker reveals their intended meaning first.
4. Each listener reveals their interpretation.
5. The group votes on match level (High / Moderate / Low) for each listener.
6. In the event of a tie, the speaker casts the deciding vote — they are the source of the intended meaning.

---

---

# Win Condition

Highest Interpretation Alignment Score.

# What It Models

MIRROR primarily examines:

U → I

The transmission layer of communication.

---

# Ideal Outcome

Players discover:

* words are compressed models
* meaning is reconstructed, not transmitted
* misunderstanding is often normal

The game rewards clarity rather than correctness.

# TRICK ENGINE GAME SPECIFICATION

# ECHO

## Version 1.0

### Classification

Epistemic Game Type:
Social Reality Game

Primary Psychological Focus:
Belief Propagation

Core Question:

> How do ideas spread through groups?

---

# Design Philosophy

ECHO explores the difference between:

* reality
* belief
* consensus

A statement may be true, false, popular, unpopular, persuasive, or ignored.

These are separate properties.

Players are rewarded for understanding the social dynamics of belief.

---

# Psychological Model

ECHO trains:

* theory of mind
* persuasion detection
* consensus prediction
* social reasoning

The central failure mode is:

> Assuming that popularity and truth are identical.

---

# Deck Characteristics

Statements should involve:

* institutions
* culture
* economics
* norms
* collective behavior

Example cards:

* Money has value because people agree it has value.
* Reputation affects outcomes.
* Nations exist independently of human belief.

---

# Core Mechanic

Before revealing reality, players predict:

1. Whether the statement is true.
2. What the majority of players will believe.

Both predictions score independently.

---

# Scoring

Correct Reality Prediction:
+2

Correct Consensus Prediction:
+2

Correct Challenge:
+2

Incorrect Challenge:
-1

---

# Win Condition

Highest combined score.

---

# What It Models

ECHO emphasizes:

R → B → U → I → L

rather than merely:

R → L

Players learn that understanding others is often as important as understanding reality.

---

# Ideal Outcome

Players become more aware of:

* groupthink
* persuasion
* social influence
* belief cascades

without confusing those phenomena with truth itself.

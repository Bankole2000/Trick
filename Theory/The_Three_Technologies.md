# The Three Technologies

The Hunter-Gatherer Magic Theory proposes that human civilization develops through three broad classes of technology.

These technologies are not independent. Each builds upon and amplifies the others.

---

## 1. Physical Technologies

Physical technologies manipulate matter and energy.

Examples:

* stone tools
* spears
* bows
* plows
* ships
* engines
* computers

Physical technologies extend the body's ability to act upon the environment.

They are extensions of:

* muscle
* movement
* force

---

## 2. Biological Technologies

Biological technologies manipulate living systems.

Examples:

* agriculture
* animal domestication
* medicine
* selective breeding
* sanitation
* vaccines

Biological technologies extend the body's ability to survive and reproduce.

They are extensions of:

* growth
* healing
* inheritance

---

## 3. Symbolic Technologies

Symbolic technologies manipulate beliefs, expectations, and coordinated behavior.

Examples:

* language
* writing
* mathematics
* money
* law
* religion
* brands
* institutions
* scientific theories

Symbolic technologies extend the mind's ability to coordinate with other minds.

They are extensions of:

* meaning
* memory
* trust
* imagination

---

# The Power Hierarchy

A single individual can affect:

* a few objects through physical technology
* many organisms through biological technology
* entire societies through symbolic technology

This is why symbolic technologies may appear magical.

A sentence can move armies.

A law can alter millions of behaviors.

A financial instrument can redirect resources across continents.

---

# Civilization as Stacked Technology

Civilizations emerge when all three technologies interact:

Physical Technology
↓
Biological Technology
↓
Symbolic Technology
↓
Large-Scale Coordination

The result is a society capable of organizing resources and behavior far beyond the capabilities of any individual animal.

---

# The Magic Interpretation

Within this framework:

* tools are physical magic
* medicine is biological magic
* language is symbolic magic

The most powerful human civilizations are those that successfully combine all three forms.

---

# Core Insight

The evolution of civilization can be interpreted as the progressive accumulation of physical, biological, and symbolic technologies.

Humans did not stop being hunter-gatherers.

Rather, they learned increasingly powerful forms of magic with which to hunt, gather, coordinate, and survive.

# THE HUNTER-GATHERER MAGIC THEORY

## A Meta-Theory of Human Civilization

### Version 0.1

---

# Central Proposition

Humans are animals in costumes playing a game of hunter-gatherer using magic tricks.

---

# Summary

The theory proposes that human civilization can be understood as an extension of animal survival behavior.

The fundamental goals remain:

* survival
* reproduction
* cooperation
* competition
* resource acquisition

What changed is the emergence of language, symbols, and abstract systems.

These systems function as a form of magic.

---

# Definitions

## Animal

A biological organism pursuing survival and reproduction.

Humans remain animals.

---

## Costume

A socially recognized identity.

Examples:

* king
* priest
* engineer
* doctor
* lawyer
* politician
* celebrity
* soldier

The costume is not the organism.

The costume is a symbolic role.

---

## Magic

The ability to alter behavior through symbols.

Examples:

* language
* stories
* laws
* money
* religions
* brands
* reputations
* institutions

A spoken sentence can cause thousands of people to act.

In this sense, words function like magic.

---

## Trick

A learned symbolic behavior that produces an outcome.

Examples:

* persuasion
* negotiation
* deception
* teaching
* marketing
* diplomacy

Human civilization is composed largely of such tricks.

---

# The Hunter-Gatherer Continuity Hypothesis

Ancient humans sought:

* food
* shelter
* mates
* allies

Modern humans seek:

* money
* status
* influence
* followers
* customers
* voters

The objects have changed.

The underlying game remains similar.

---

# Costumes and Civilization

Civilizations are networks of costumes.

A costume grants:

* authority
* trust
* expectations
* responsibilities

The same biological organism may wear many costumes throughout life.

---

# Magic Systems

Human societies construct shared magical systems.

Examples:

## Money

Money has power because people collectively believe in it.

---

## Law

Laws alter behavior through symbolic agreement.

---

## Religion

Religious systems organize behavior through shared narratives.

---

## Nations

Nations are maintained through collective belief in symbols and stories.

Language acts as a reality-modification technology.

# Core Claim

Civilization is not separate from nature.

Rather:

> civilization is nature using symbols.

Human institutions are extensions of biological behavior expressed through increasingly sophisticated symbolic systems.

---

# The Grand Game

The theory views society as a nested hierarchy of games:

```text
Survival
    ↓
Hunter-Gatherer
    ↓
Language
    ↓
Institutions
    ↓
Civilization
```

Every layer introduces new tricks.

Every trick creates new possibilities.

---

# Implications

If the theory is correct:

* social roles are costumes
* institutions are symbolic constructions
* language is a form of behavioral technology
* trust is a resource
* reputation is a resource
* information is a resource
* civilization is a symbolic extension of animal behavior

---

# Final Statement

Humans are animals.

They learned words.

Words became magic.

Magic became institutions.

Institutions became civilizations.

And civilization became an elaborate game of hunter-gatherer played through costumes, symbols, stories, and tricks.
